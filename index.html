The Dead Dummies

Here we remember those that fell in the Fey. Their deaths were not noble, nor were they courageous. They did not fall with grace, nor did they meet their end with songs waiting to be sung. They will most likey be forgotten where they rot. The DM did say they came back at the end, but we all know that was a lie. A lie like you would tell ahcild so they do not cry about their lost toy. This page is to remember them. Not as heroes, but as echoes of lives once lived.

Below will tell the tales of those who have fallen.

Ivellious

"No one trusted him, and while napping in a tree, he was forgotten. Panicked, he chased after his crew—only to get wrecked by a jabberwock like the fool he was."

Haggin

"They say you miss every shot you don’t take—Haggin missed every shot he did take. The one time he finally landed a hit... it was the last thing he ever did."

Obsidian

"Probably died in a ditch somewhere still pissed he didn't swing at Pockets."

Thorin

"Tough as the anvil you would shape steel on... friend to many, nightmare to his enemies. The one none of us will forget."

Aeson

"Like many paladins, he looked stacked—though it may have just been the armor. Weaker than a skeleton’s rotted biceps, he died doing what he did best: healing the enemy."

"Their tale will echo through time—mostly as a cautionary tale, a warning not to get involved."

— Local Bard
